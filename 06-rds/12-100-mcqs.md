**Selected explanations**
- **3:** RDS is managed — no OS/SSH; tune via parameter groups.
- **10/14:** Storage only grows (autoscaling included); can't shrink in place.
- **17/18:** Multi-AZ standby is synchronous and **not readable** — that's what replicas are for.
- **35/42:** A replica *can* be promoted, which **breaks** replication into a standalone primary.
- **55/56:** PITR needs automated backups (retention > 0); restores to ~5 min of now, to a new instance.
- **61:** KMS keys are regional → cross-region copy must re-encrypt with a target-Region key.
- **92:** No in-place encryption — snapshot, copy-with-encryption, restore.
- **100:** Public instance + `0.0.0.0/0` SG is the classic breach path.
